Welcome, Admin!

Here’s a brief overview of your EV Fleet. You can access detailed vehicle performance data by exploring the sections above.

Vehicle ID

Each vehicle in the fleet is uniquely identified by an ID. This helps distinguish between different EVs for easy reference and management.

Make

The Make of the EV refers to the manufacturer or brand of the vehicle, helping you understand its origin and the quality standards it adheres to.

Driver

The Driver column shows the person assigned to drive the vehicle, ensuring accountability and proper assignment within the fleet.

Manager

This indicates the Manager overseeing the specific vehicle’s operations, ensuring efficient management and maintenance of the fleet.

Total Power (hp)

This refers to the total power output of the vehicle’s motor(s), indicating the overall performance capacity in terms of horsepower.

Range

The Range column shows how far the vehicle can travel on a single charge. This is a critical parameter for fleet management to ensure long-distance travel and timely recharging.

Battery Health

The Battery Health column provides insights into the current condition of the vehicle's battery. Monitoring battery health is essential for preventing sudden breakdowns and for planning battery replacement before performance drops.

Charging Status

The Charging Status column indicates whether the vehicle is currently charging and its progress. Keeping track of the charging status ensures that vehicles are fully charged when needed and helps optimize fleet utilization.

Total Torque

Total Torque refers to the combined rotational force produced by the vehicle's motor(s). It determines the vehicle's ability to accelerate, tow heavy loads, and perform under demanding conditions. For electric vehicles, torque is delivered instantly, ensuring swift and efficient performance.

Acceleration

Acceleration indicates how quickly a vehicle can increase its speed, usually measured as time taken to reach a certain velocity (e.g., 0-60 mph). It is directly influenced by total torque and reflects the vehicle's responsiveness and overall performance dynamics.
